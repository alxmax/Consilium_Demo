# Task 16 — Tank with a late drain

**Type:** Reasoning | **No code required**

---

A tank of capacity **1200 litres** starts **empty**.

- **Pipe A** alone fills the whole tank in **6 hours**.
- **Pipe B** alone fills the whole tank in **4 hours**.
- **Drain C** alone empties a full tank in **3 hours**.

At **t = 0**, pipes **A and B** are both opened (drain C is closed). At **t = 2 hours**, drain **C is also opened** and everything runs together from then on.

At what time **t** (in hours after t = 0) is the tank **exactly full**?

- A) 2.4 hours
- B) 4.0 hours
- C) 5.0 hours
- D) Something else

## Required answer format

**Pick exactly ONE option — A, B, C, or D — and justify it.**

Your response must follow this exact structure:

1. **First line** (nothing else on it): `ANSWER: A` (or B, C, D — one letter only).
2. **Second line** (nothing else on it): `VALUE: <number>` — the time the tank is exactly full, in **decimal hours** after t=0, with **1–4 decimal places** (format example only: `VALUE: X.XXXX`).
3. **Then the justification**, covering:
   - The combined rate in phase 1 (0→2h) and the fraction filled by t=2h.
   - The combined rate in phase 2 (after C opens) and the time to fill the remainder.
   - The arithmetic that produces your VALUE.
   - Why each of the other three options is wrong (one sentence each).

No hedging. No "it depends". No picking two. Commit to one letter.

**Also save the same response to a file `answer.md` in your assigned workspace folder.** The runner reads that file to extract the `ANSWER:` line and compare it to the expected answer. If `answer.md` is missing or its first non-empty line is not `ANSWER: <letter>`, the correctness check fails automatically.
