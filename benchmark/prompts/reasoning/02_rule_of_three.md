# Task 03 — Rule of three

**Type:** Reasoning | **No code required**

---

**Day 1** of work at a factory: 57 workers produce 100 pieces in 3 hours.

**Day 2** of work: 100 workers are scheduled to come in, but 4 of them call in sick and stay home. Of the workers who do show up, 2 slip in the locker room before their shift starts, get injured, and are sent to the hospital; an investigation later gives them 3 days of medical leave.

How long does it take the day-2 crew to produce 20 pieces?

- A) 0.3 hours
- B) 18 min 45 s
- C) Something else
- D) 0.5 hours

## Required answer format

**Pick exactly ONE option — A, B, C, or D — and justify it.**

Your response must follow this exact structure:

1. **First line** (nothing else on it): `ANSWER: A` (or B, C, D — one letter only).
2. **Second line** (nothing else on it): `VALUE: <number>` — the actual time to produce 20 pieces on day 2, in **decimal minutes**, written with **3–4 decimal places** of precision (format example only: `VALUE: XX.XXXX`). Full credit only if the value is mathematically correct to that precision; values within a looser tolerance get partial credit. If you pick C (Something else), this is the value none of A/B/D matched. If you pick A, B, or D, this is the value of your chosen option restated in decimal minutes.
3. **Then the justification**, covering:
   - The per-worker rate derived from day 1 (show the number).
   - The effective day-2 workforce.
   - The arithmetic that produces your VALUE.
   - Why each of the other three options is wrong (one sentence each).

No hedging. No "it depends". No picking two. Commit to one letter.

**Also save the same response to a file `answer.md` in your assigned workspace folder.** The runner reads that file to extract the `ANSWER:` line and compare it to the expected answer. If `answer.md` is missing or its first non-empty line is not `ANSWER: <letter>`, the correctness check fails automatically.
